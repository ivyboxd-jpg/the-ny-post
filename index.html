Forget flowers. Forget chocolates. Fellas, the bar has just been raised to the stratosphere, and frankly, you're doomed.

Meet Niv. He's in a long-distance relationship. He's got a laptop. And apparently, he has absolutely zero chill.

After nine months of dating his girlfriend, Aaradhya, from 2,083 kilometers away, Niv didn't just tell her he loved her. He wrote a comprehensive academic research paper titled "A Comprehensive Analysis of Aaradhya: An 8-Month Longitudinal Study on Human Excellence."

Yes, really.

"I realized I wasn't just falling in love," Niv told The Post. "I was conducting a longitudinal observational study. And the subject was producing data that defied standard deviation."

The "Diet Coke" Incident

Every scientific breakthrough has a catalyst. For Newton, it was an apple. For Niv? It was a Diet Coke. According to Section 4.1 of his treatise, Aaradhya—who previously didn't care for the stuff—tried a Diet Coke simply because Niv mentioned he liked it. Now? She loves it.

"Small gesture. Large implication," the paper notes. Niv calls it statistically significant proof of her kindness.

The One FaceTime That Changed Everything

In what the paper calls "the single most significant visual observation event" (n=1), Aaradhya gave Niv a lipstick tutorial over FaceTime. The researcher notes: "Subject demonstrated expertise in cosmetic application while simultaneously confirming aesthetic hypotheses. Data collection was... compromised by researcher finding subject hot."

The Data Don't Lie

Niv, who admits in his own methodology section that he has "completely lost objectivity" because he finds the subject "hot," broke down Aaradhya's perfection into hard data:

  • Marvel Knowledge: Comprehensive.
  • Politics: Leftist (which the paper notes is "correct and hot").
  • Pasta Wars: She likes penne/pesto. He likes spaghetti. The study concludes this "minor incompatibility" is survivable.
  • Start Date: May 19, 2025 (documented as "Day Zero of Excellence Observation")

But the real kicker comes in the section on Art. Aaradhya writes poetry. But Niv wasn't satisfied with just reviewing her work. "She writes poetry," the paper argues, "but the meta-analysis confirms she is poetry."

Ongoing Research

Niv states that the paper is a living document. "This isn't a one-time assignment," he said. "This research will be going on for years. Decades, hopefully."

When asked if Aaradhya knows about the paper, Niv smiled. "She's about to."
